Healthcare Media Fairness and Bias

Healthcare :

AI in Medical Diagnosis Artificial Intelligence (AI) has shown significant promise in the field of medical diagnosis. AI algorithms can analyze vast amounts of data to provide faster and more accurate integration of patient biometrics, images, medical records, genomic data, and clinical notes1. For example, AI in diagnosis can analyze medical images, such as X-rays, MRIs, and CT scans, to identify abnormalities and assist healthcare professionals in making accurate diagnoses in mere seconds2. AI is also being used to diagnose diseases and identify the best treatment plans for patients facing critical illnesses like cancer.

AI in Treatment Recommendation AI has a transformative role in recommending treatments. AI-enabled patient recommendation engines intelligently filter data and use the relevant information to analyze patient symptoms, assist in precise diagnosis, suggest personalized treatment plans to patients, and predict future health risks for them4. AI algorithms analyze patient-specific data, such as medical history, genetic information, lifestyle factors, and treatment responses of similar patients, to generate personalized treatment recommendations5. AI is not a replacement for physicians, but it supports their decisions and makes recommendations based on analyses of large amounts of healthcare data such as electronic health records (EHRs), symptom data, medical images, doctor reports, and more.

Fairness and Bias :

Fairness and Bias in AI are two sides of the same coin that have significant implications on the outcomes of AI systems12. Bias can be introduced at any stage in the model development pipeline and is inherently present in the world around us1. It can show up in several forms, such as historical bias, representation bias, and measurement bias1. On the other hand, fairness in AI can be broadly defined as the absence of prejudice or preference for an individual or group based on their characteristics1. AI fairness aims to alleviate such biases, preventing discrimination3. However, achieving fairness in AI is a complex task that requires a clear understanding of bias and fairness, along with the implementation of effective mitigation strategies124. The goal is to develop AI systems that help make decisions leading to fair and equitable outcomes.
